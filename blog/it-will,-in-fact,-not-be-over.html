It will, in fact, not be over

27/6/2026

At this point it's quite apparent that I have a slight issue with catastrophizing certain outcomes whenever they fall out of my control. It comes with the clear disadvantage of a significant amount of anxiety, but it has also taught me how to deal with failure without actually failing.

I've already written about how this played out with my participation in IPhO 2026, and it has just happened again with the latin american astronomy olympiad, since Paraguay stepped in and is going to be the one to fill the host gap this year. Regardless, my focus still lies on physics, and at the moment of writing this, 7 days away from the competition, I don't recall ever feeling so lucky to have my life.

I really want to do well. A big chunk of my day is spent trying to be at my best for my last dance, which is looking like it's gonna be a good shot at achieving what I once desperately dreamt about when I started this whole journey. But I also know that, no matter how many hours I spend, I have absolutely no way to guarantee any outcome. All I can do is improve my odds, but it's quite likely that I've finally reached the point of diminishing returns and I might not be able to improve that much even if provided with obscene amounts of extra time (at least when it comes to the theory exam, as my experimental will most likely be held by the almighty force of hope and prayers).

And it's alright, I'm quite happy with the improvement I've made with respect to last year. One has to remember why even bother to attempt getting a shiny metal disk. I think that many of us have, at some point, lost the notion of competitions actually just being a ridiculously overpowered tool to drive yourself and get to improve certain very useful skills, with the added bonus of learning a ton of stuff and meeting some wonderful people along the way. Regardless of how the first week of July goes for me, I'll be forever grateful for discovering that this whole world of olympiads exists.

Life will go on after the exams, whether the result is euphoric or not. There's a wonderfully vast array of things that can be done and learnt later down the line, I'll move on, and it will be far from over.
